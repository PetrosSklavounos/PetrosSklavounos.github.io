Design and Analysis of a Wind Turbine with FEA ANSYS Software

Objective

The aim of the project was to design and analyze a wind turbine using FEA ANSYS software, optimizing parameters for the most effective design.

Software

The design and analysis were performed using ANSYS Workbench. The software allowed for parametrization and automated iterations of simulations.

Skills Learned

This project improved skills in design and analysis using ANSYS Workbench, parametrization, automation of simulations, and nodal analysis.

Design and Assumptions

The design was optimized by automatically running over 100 iterations of simulations. The raw output was exported to a CSV, and filters were applied to ensure the design requirements were met. The optimal iteration was selected for minimal tower mass, ensuring efficient material usage. A nodal analysis of the first 6 modes was conducted on the tower using pre-stress conditions taken from the static structural solution.

Project Results

The final design ensured that each natural frequency calculated did not fall into the frequency range of the spinning blades. This was achieved by cross-checking each natural frequency across the 6 environmental cases considered for a single design iteration.
