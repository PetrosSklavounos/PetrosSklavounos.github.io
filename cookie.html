Custom Cookie Cutter Manufacturing Project

Objective

Design and manufacture a custom cookie cutter with a specified degree of precision. The process involves designing the cutter, assembling the necessary tools, and creating metal blanks for bending into the final cookie cutter shape chosen. The project culminates in analyzing the final cookie cutters and the cookies made with these cutters to determine if the tolerancing was met.

Software Used

CNC Machine Interface and Onshape, coupled with Ultimaker Cura.

Skills Learned

This project enhanced skills in CAD design, CNC machining, and manual assembly techniques including drilling, tapping, and using hex bit sockets for precise construction.

Manufacturing Process

  • Phase 1: CAD modeling of the cutter, mold, and press dies.
  • Phase 2: CNC-cutting of aluminum mold and press dies, with pilot holes for manual threading.
  • Phase 3: Assembly with precision drilling and securing components for even load distribution.
  • Phase 4: Reinforcing the structure against bending forces using epoxy and wooden beams.

Final Product Demonstration

<

The final cookie cutters met the client's 1/8 inch deviation tolerance in all dimensions, demonstrating high design quality, functionality, and accuracy.
