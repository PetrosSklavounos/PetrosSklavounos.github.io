OPTIMIZING CO2 SENSOR PLACEMENT IN 41 COOPER CLASSROOMS VIA CFD AND SENSOR DATA ANALYTICS

Objective

To assess and optimize CO2 sensor locations in Room 506 at 41 Cooper Square. By employing Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) simulations alongside empirical data, the study seeks to identify the most representative sensor position for capturing the volumetric bulk average of CO2 concentration. This refined placement will enable more accurate measurements for the building management system (BMS), ensuring effective ventilation control and maintaining optimal indoor air quality for enhanced student health and cognitive performance.

Software Used

Ansys Workbench and Solidworks Flow Simulation were used for the Computational Fluid Dynamics Simulations of the transient carbon dioxide concentration and meshing .

Skills Learned

The project enhanced skills in Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) simulations run specifically in Solidworks as Ansys Fluent was what we were initially familiar. This gave a good basis for comparison on which software to use according to the problem at hand. Data analysis techniques were learned along the way when using the sensor data and exported results from the simulations for optimizing CO2 sensor placement in classroom environments, whether it was developing python scripts for sorting and de-noising data, to identifying certain patterns and traits, as well as visualizing the data and its implications.Additionally, skills were developed in critical thinking when hypothesizing and validating sensor placement strategies by using analogous thought experiments.

Methodology

Experimental Methodology:

  1. Calibration: Calibration of HOBO MX and Particles Plus 8302 sensors outdoors to establish baseline CO2 levels.
  2. Sensor Placement: Strategic placement of sensors in Room 506, taking into account variations in room occupancy and external environmental factors.
  3. Data Collection: Systematic recording of CO2 levels during various class sessions, focusing on different sensor locations for comprehensive data coverage.
  4. Data Extraction: Aggregating and analyzing sensor data post-class sessions, with comparison to the Building Management System's (BMS) readings of the CO2.

Computational Methodology:

  1. Simulation Setup: Employing SolidWorks Fluid Flow Simulations for detailed modeling of CO2 gas flow dynamics in Room 506.
  2. Meshing and Domain Adjustment: Precise configuration of computational domain and meshing parameters within SolidWorks for accurate simulation.
  3. Initial Conditions Setting: Establishing specific initial simulation parameters, including environmental conditions and CO2 sources and concentrations for exhausted air or makeup air.
  4. Data Analysis: In-depth analysis of simulation results, focusing on identifying the most effective sensor placement for accurate CO2 monitoring.
  5. Post-Processing: Utilizing advanced Python and Excel techniques for further data refinement and analysis to validate simulation results.
