Principles of HVAC Air Curtains and the Impact of Mass Flow Rate on Thermal Barrier Effectiveness

December 20, 2024

Documentation

Download Report

Objective

This study investigates the thermal and fluid dynamic behavior of air curtains as a solution for maintaining thermal barriers between adjacent environments. Through computational fluid dynamics (CFD) simulations, the project aims to evaluate the effectiveness of varying mass flow rates in minimizing thermal infiltration while balancing energy consumption and performance.

Summary

This study demonstrated that higher air curtain mass flow rates significantly reduced thermal infiltration, achieving superior thermal separation but at the cost of increased energy consumption. Lower flow rates provided a more energy-efficient solution while still maintaining moderate thermal isolation. The findings highlighted a trade-off between performance and energy efficiency, underscoring the importance of optimal configuration for various applications.

Skills Learned

Through this project, I developed expertise in computational fluid dynamics (CFD) using ANSYS Fluent, focusing on steady-state simulations and the analysis of fluid flow and heat transfer. Key techniques included implementing boundary conditions, analyzing jet dynamics, and optimizing flow configurations for energy efficiency and thermal performance.

I enhanced my ability to preprocess and post-process simulation data, utilizing Python and MATLAB for tasks such as mesh refinement, temperature contour analysis, and data visualization. I also learned advanced turbulence modeling, applying the SST k-ω model to accurately capture boundary layer effects and shear flows.

Additionally, I acquired skills in numerical methods for solving Navier-Stokes and energy equations, alongside the use of validation metrics such as the Deflection Modulus and Reynolds number to evaluate thermal and aerodynamic performance. Visualization techniques, including generating temperature and velocity contour plots, further improved my ability to present complex simulation results effectively.

Methodology

The analysis utilized computational fluid dynamics simulations with steady-state conditions to evaluate three scenarios: high, low, and no mass flow rates for air curtains. Parameters such as mass flow rate, temperature gradients, and recirculation dynamics were analyzed using the SST k-ω turbulence model. Data for boundary conditions and fluid properties were sourced from academic literature and validated using established equations and experimental findings.

Discussion and Conclusion

This study examined the thermal and fluid dynamic performance of air curtains as a thermal barrier between adjacent spaces, utilizing computational fluid dynamics (CFD) simulations. The results demonstrated that higher mass flow rates significantly reduced thermal infiltration, achieving superior temperature gradients and minimizing mixing between warm and cool regions. The Deflection Modulus calculations supported these findings, showing improved aerodynamic stability at higher flow rates.

However, the energy efficiency analysis revealed a substantial trade-off: the high mass flow scenario consumed 216 times more energy than the low flow scenario while only achieving a modest additional temperature reduction of 1.38°C in the second room. This highlights the importance of balancing performance with operational cost when configuring air curtains for different applications.

The study also uncovered the limitations of low flow rates in maintaining effective thermal barriers due to insufficient jet momentum and increased susceptibility to recirculation and turbulence. These effects were observed through temperature contour plots and streamline visualizations, which showed significant warm air infiltration in low-flow and no-flow scenarios.

Future work could focus on optimizing nozzle geometries, integrating adaptive flow systems, and incorporating transient environmental conditions to improve air curtain efficiency. Experimental validation of the CFD results would further enhance reliability and provide practical insights for real-world implementation.
