Hemodialysis Needle Redesign Project

May 15, 2024

Objective

This project aims to redesign hemodialysis needles to improve patient comfort and compliance. The new needle design transitions from rigid to flexible at body temperature, minimizing tissue trauma and discomfort during insertion and use.

Its abstract has been accepted by the ASME IMECE conference, and the draft submission status is pending.

Materials and Methods

The needle is made from a blend of Poly(methyl methacrylate) (PMMA), Polypropylene carbonate (PPC), and Polyvinyl acetate (PVAc). These polymers are biocompatible and hemocompatible, and they do not polymerize when mixed. The thermal time constant for the needle to reach the glass phase temperature is about 6 seconds, ensuring quick adaptation to body temperature during insertion.

The manufacturing process includes CNC machining and injection molding. An aluminum injection mold is used to fabricate components with geometries mimicking those of 15-gauge needles. Ansys Workbench was used for Finite Element Analysis (FEA), and SolidWorks was used for CAD modeling.

Material Testing

Tensile testing revealed that the polymer composite has a tensile modulus of 1.8 GPa at room temperature, decreasing to 135 MPa at body temperature. This significant drop in tensile modulus ensures the needle maintains sufficient rigidity for insertion and becomes flexible enough to conform to the vein, reducing mechanical mismatch and potential injury.

Experimental Setup

The PDMS phantoms were designed to assess the insertion dynamics and vascular interaction of hemodialysis needles under various conditions. Vein and AVF phantoms were used to simulate different anatomical scenarios and evaluate the needle's performance during insertion at different angles and radii.
