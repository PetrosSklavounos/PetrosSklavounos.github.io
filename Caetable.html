Executive Stone Desk Design - Computer Aided Engineering Analysis

Objective

This project aimed to design an executive stone-topped desk with dimensions and materials capable of withstanding specified load parameters.

Software

The design was modeled and analyzed using ANSYS APDL to ensure deformation and stress resistance, adhering to design constraints under regular usage.

Skills Learned

This project enhanced skills in design optimization, understanding and usage of materials, handling load parameters, and using ANSYS software for real-world applications.

Design and Assumptions

Iterations were made to optimize table legs, crossbeams, material, and support plate thickness. Simplifications were made in the model for efficiency and to avoid singularities or truncation errors. The construction of the tabletop and support plate were assumed to be glued together.

Project Results

The final design featured Stainless Steel 304 square beams (0.08m x 0.08m) for legs and crossbeams, an Aluminum 6061 support plate (0.03m thickness) under an 18mm granite tabletop. The design met stress (FOS: 14) and buckling (FOS: 4.85) safety factors, withstanding its own weight and a 1112N point load on the table surface.
