VIBRATION PROJECT: MODELING COOPER HYPERLOOP'S HORIZONTAL STABILITY

Documentation

3D Model

Objective

To enhance the efficiency and safety of Hyperloop pod operations, our team constructed a scaled testing rig to analyze and mitigate disruptions caused by bumps along the I-beam track.

Method

The rig, representing a 2-degree of freedom system, emulates an uneven track and utilizes a trailing arm suspension, stepper motor, and Arduino microcontroller. Through a Scotch yoke linkage mechanism, the motor generates oscillating vertical motion, resulting in compression and decompression of a linkage arm connected to the top of the system. This enables the evaluation of spring dynamics and facilitates the gathering of empirical data on displacement and performance using Tracker software.

Software & Hardware

The construction process made use of advanced techniques and tools offered in the AACE lab including the Fablight 4500 for metal laser cutting, a waterjet cutter for the metal masses, and a laser cutter for precise cuts on the plywood. We also incorporated 3D printing for complex structures like the 3D printed knob, designed with Onshape CAD software. Rhino software was used to interface with the machines listed.

Limitations
